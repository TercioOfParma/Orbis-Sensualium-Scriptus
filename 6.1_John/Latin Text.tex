\section{Intended Audience}
This is intended for students who have completed Lectio 9, 10, 11 and 12 of Latin by the Natural Method and Chapter VII of Lingua Latina Per Se Illustrata with the Colloquium. Although there is a lot of subjunctives here, they have been glossed. There are 533 words in this chapter.

\section{Text}

Quod fuit ab initiō, quod audīvimus, quod vīdimus oculīs nostrīs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = With our eyes}, quod perspeximus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We have looked upon}, et manūs nostrae contrectāvērunt\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We have handled} dē verbō vītae:

 et vīta manifestāta est, et vīdimus, et testāmur\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We testify}, et annūntiāmus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We announce} vōbīs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = To you (plural)} vītam aeternam, quae erat apud\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = With (this takes the accusative)} Patrem, et appāruit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = It has appeared} nōbīs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = To us}\: quod vīdimus et audīvimus, annūntiāmus vōbīs, ut et vōs societātem habeātis nōbīscum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = So that you also may have society with us}, et societās nostra sit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = It may be} cum Patre, et cum Fīliō ejus Jēsū Chrīstō. Et haec scrībimus vōbīs ut gaudeātis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = So that you may be rejoice}, et gaudium vestrum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Your joy} sit plēnum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Full}. Et haec est annūntiātiō\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Declaration}, quam audīvimus ab eō, et annūntiāmus vōbīs: quoniam\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = That, since} Deus lūx est, et tenebrae in eō nōn sunt ūllae\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Any}. Sī dīxerīmus quoniam societātem habēmus cum eō, et in tenebrīs ambulāmus, mentīmur\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We are lying}, et vēritātem\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = The truth} nōn facimus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We do}. Sī autem in lūce ambulāmus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We are walking} sīcut et ipse\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He himself} est in lūce, societātem habēmus ad invicem\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = One another}, et sanguis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Blood} Jēsū Chrīstī, Fīliī ejus, ēmundat\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = It washes} nōs ab omnī peccātō. Sī dīxerīmus quoniam peccātum nōn habēmus, ipsī\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = The same (in this case we)} nōs sēdūcimus, et vēritās in nōbīs nōn est. Sī cōnfiteāmur\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We would confess} peccāta nostra\: fidēlis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Faithful} est, et jūstus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Just}, ut remittat nōbīs peccāta nostra\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = So that he remits to us our sins}, et ēmundet\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He cleans (This is subjunctive like remittat)} nōs ab omnī inīquitāte. Sī dīxerīmus quoniam nōn peccāvimus, mendācem\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Liar} facimus eum, et verbum ejus nōn est in nōbīs. Fīliolī meī\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = My little children}, haec scrībō vōbīs, ut nōn peccētis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = So that you may not sin}. Sed et sī quis peccāverit, advocātum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Advocate} habēmus apud Patrem, Jēsum Chrīstum jūstum\: et ipse est propitiātiō\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Propitiation} prō\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = For} peccātīs nostrīs: nōn prō nostrīs autem tantum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Only, Alone}, sed etiam prō tōtīus mundī.
 Et in hōc scīmus quoniam cognōvimus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We have known him (In a more experiential rather than intellectual way)} eum, sī mandāta ejus observēmus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We keep}. Quī dīcit sē\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Himself, Itself, Herself} nōsse\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = To know (this is a perfect infinitive)} eum, et mandāta ejus nōn cūstōdit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He guards}, mendāx est, et in hōc vēritās nōn est. Quī autem servat\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He would serve} verbum ejus, vērē\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Truly} in hōc cāritās\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Divine Love, Agape} Deī perfecta\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Fulfilled} est: et in hōc scīmus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We know} quoniam in ipsō\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = In him (In the same, that is, the Lord)} sumus. Quī dīcit sē in ipsō manēre\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = To remain}, dēbet, sīcut ille\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He} ambulāvit, et ipse ambulāre. Cārissimī\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Beloved(s)}, nōn mandātum novum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = New} scrībō vōbīs, sed mandātum vetus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Old (Vetus doesn't change its ending so it's an old commandment, or mandatum vetus)}, quod habuistis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You(plural) have had} ab initiō. Mandātum vetus est verbum, quod audīstis.Iterum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Again} mandātum novum scrībō vōbīs, quod vērum est et in ipsō, et in vōbīs: quia tenebrae trānsiērunt\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = They (or, it since tenebrae is always plural darkness) have passed away}, et vērum lūmen jam\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Now} lūcet. Quī dīcit sē in lūce esse, et frātrem suum ōdit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He hates}, in tenebrīs est usque\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = until} adhūc\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = to this point}. Quī dīligit frātrem suum, in lūmine manet, et scandalum in eō nōn est.
Quī autem ōdit frātrem suum, in tenebrīs est, et in tenebrīs ambulat, et nescit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He doesn't know} quō eat\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He would go}: quia tenebrae obcaecāvērunt\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = They (it) has blinded} oculōs ejus. Scrībō vōbīs, fīliolī, quoniam remittuntur vōbīs peccāta propter\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Because} nōmen ejus. Scrībō vōbīs, patrēs, quoniam cognōvistis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You have thought} eum, quī ab initiō est. Scrībō vōbīs, adolēscentēs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Young men}, quoniam vīcistis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You have conquered} malignum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = The evil one}. Scrībō vōbīs, īnfantēs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Babes}, quoniam cognōvistis patrem. Scrībō vōbīs juvenēs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Young men}, quoniam fortēs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Strong (plural)} estis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You are (plural)}, et verbum Deī manet in vōbīs, et vīcistis malignum. Nōlīte dīligere\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Don't Love!} mundum, neque\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Nor} ea quae in mundō sunt. Sī quis dīligit mundum, nōn est cāritās Patrīs in eō: quoniam omne quod est in mundō, concupiscentia carnis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Concupisence of the Flesh} est, et concupiscentia oculōrum, et superbia vītae\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = The pride of life}: quae nōn est ex Patre, sed ex mundō est. Et mundus trānsit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = It passes away}, et concupiscentia ejus: quī autem facit voluntātem Deī manet in aeternum.
 Fīliolī, novissima\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Final} hōra\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Hour} est: et sīcut audīstis quia\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Because, That} antichristus venit, et nunc antichristī multī factī sunt; unde scīmus, quia novissima hōra est. Ex\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = From} nōbīs prōdiērunt\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = They went out}, sed nōn erant\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = They were} ex nōbīs, nam\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Because}, sī fuissent\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = If they were} ex nōbīs, permānsissent\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = They would have remained} utique\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Certainly} nōbīscum: sed ut manifēstī sint\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = So that they may be manifest} quoniam nōn sunt omnēs ex nōbīs. Sed vōs ūnctiōnem\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Anointing} habētis ā Sānctō, et nōstis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You have known} omnia. Nōn scrīpsī\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = I have written} vōbīs quasi\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = As if} ignōrantibus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = To those who are ignorant} vēritātem, sed quasi scientibus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = To those knowing} eam: et quoniam omne mendācium ex vēritāte nōn est. Quis est mendāx, nisi\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = If not} is quī negat\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He denies} quoniam Jēsus est Chrīstus? Hic est antichristus, quī negat Patrem, et Fīlium. Omnis quī negat Fīlium, nec Patrem habet: quī cōnfitētur Fīlium, et Patrem habet.
Vōs quod audīstis ab initiō, in vōbīs permaneat\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Let it remain}: sī in vōbīs permānserit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = It will abide} quod audīstis ab initiō, et vōs in Fīliō et Patre manēbitis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You will remain}.

 Et haec est reprōmissiō\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Promise}, quam ipse pollicitus est\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = He has promised} nōbīs, vītam aeternam.

 Haec scrīpsī vōbīs dē hīs, quī sēdūcant\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = They would seduce} vōs.

 Et vōs ūnctiōnem, quam accēpistis ab eō, maneat\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Let it remain} in vōbīs. Et nōn necesse habētis ut aliquis doceat vōs\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = That anybody would teach you (That is, from the seducers, you have your knowledge from the church}: sed sīcut ūnctiō ejus docet vōs dē omnibus, et vērum\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = True} est, et nōn est mendācium. Et sīcut docuit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = It taught} vōs: manēte\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Remain!} in eō.
 Et nunc\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Now}, fīliolī, manēte in eō: ut cum appāruerit\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = So that when he appears}, habeāmus\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Let us have} fīdūciam\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Confidence}, et nōn cōnfundāmur\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = We be confounded} ab eō in adventū\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Coming} ejus.

 Sī scītis\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = You (plural) know} quoniam jūstus est, scītōte\footnote{\textbf{Vēnistī} = Know!} quoniam et omnis, quī facit jūstitiam, ex ipsō nātus est.
